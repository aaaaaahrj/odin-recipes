Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a rich and creamy Italian pasta dish made with just a few simple ingredients. The key to an authentic Carbonara is using high-quality eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, crispy pancetta, and freshly cracked black pepper. The pasta is cooked to al dente and then tossed with a silky sauce made from eggs and cheese, which is gently heated by the warmth of the pasta, creating a smooth, creamy coating without the need for heavy cream.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz (340g) spaghetti
  • 4 oz (115g) pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta over medium heat until crispy. Remove the pancetta from the skillet and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the skillet.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and black pepper until well combined.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet with the rendered pancetta fat and toss to coat. Remove the skillet from the heat and quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing the pasta continuously to create a creamy sauce. The residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and melt the cheese, creating a smooth and creamy sauce.
  5. Add the crispy pancetta back to the skillet and toss to combine. Serve the Spaghetti Carbonara hot with additional Pecorino Romano cheese and black pepper.
  6. Enjoy your delicious homemade Spaghetti Carbonara with a side of garlic bread and a fresh green salad.
